Torah AI – Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us.

Last updated: April 24, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Torah AI, developed by Burak Güvenç ("we", "us", or "our") handles your information when you use the Torah AI mobile application.

1. Information We Collect

Torah AI is designed to respect your privacy. We collect only the minimum information necessary to provide our services:

  • Push notification token: Used solely to deliver Torah study reminders and updates you have opted into. We use OneSignal to manage notifications; your token is never sold or shared with third parties.
  • Usage data: Anonymous, aggregated analytics (e.g. crash reports) collected via standard platform SDKs to improve app stability.
  • Subscription status: Stored locally on your device only. We do not transmit or store your purchase history on our servers.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, or any personally identifiable information.
  • We do not collect location data.
  • We do not collect contacts, photos, or any device data beyond what is listed above.
  • We do not sell your data to third parties.

3. Third-Party Services

Torah AI uses the following third-party services, each governed by their own privacy policy:

  • OpenAI – Powers AI-generated Torah explanations and commentary. Queries are sent to OpenAI's API. See: openai.com/privacy
  • OneSignal – Push notification delivery. See: onesignal.com/privacy_policy
  • Google AdMob – Displays ads to non-premium users. AdMob may collect device identifiers for ad personalization. See: policies.google.com/privacy
  • Sefaria – Provides Torah text and commentary data. See: sefaria.org/terms

4. Data Retention

All user preferences (saved verses, reading streak, notification settings) are stored exclusively on your device using local storage. We have no access to this data. Uninstalling the app permanently removes all locally stored data.

5. Children's Privacy

Torah AI is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
